About us

A Free Bird™ is a 501(c)3 nonprofit, arts education organization dedicated to helping young people, ages 4-18, affected by cancer, fight their disease through the therapeutic benefits of the arts. We do this primarily through progressive, three-stage programming that integrates art therapy with art education and facilitates access to the arts and artistic expression. Our three core programs introduce children to and inspire them to explore the arts (Dream); match children with artists to educate them in a creative expression of their choice (Fight Free) and celebrate children as artists by providing them with opportunities to experience life in the artistic field of their choosing, through an exciting day of total immersion (Free & Free Pro).

A Free Bird believes in the immense power of the arts, and is committed to spreading its healing properties to as many children affected by cancer as possible. We believe too, that children are natural artists, and when provided with the right guidance, will demonstrate exceptional talent. To this end, we offer artistic resources in all art forms, and make them available to children with cancer, their parents and hospitals.

The artistic careers of our young artists are jump-started and nourished through our three core programs: Dream (inspires children);Fight Free (educates children); and Free & Free Pro (celebrates children). We work closely with museums, dance and theatre companies, art schools and local universities, as well as local artists, musicians, actors, art students and other partners to provide art tutors to children, organize and host arts related activities at hospitals, and create opportunities for artistic growth even when cancer is in remission. In addition to our artistic partners, we also receive support from foundations and corporations, whose generous donations help us develop and maintain our programming.

A Free Bird was founded and established in New York City in 2008, and became a 501(c)3 tax-exempt organization in January 2011.We are headquartered in New York-New Jersey, and are currently building a national and global reach in our effort to provide aid for children undergoing medical treatment around the world. This year we are planning to open up a branch office in Israel.

A Free Bird is entirely volunteer-based with 100% of all donations and fundraising proceeds going directly towards programming. We were recently recognized by Mayor Bill DeBlasio and NYC Service as one of the city’s most engaged organizations for volunteer service.

Mission

Our mission is to help children discover the artist within, shape them as artists, and empower each child to fly free.

Vision

Every day, 43 children are diagnosed with cancer in the United States. Our vision is to reach each of these children in the early stages of cancer, and inspire them to find the artist within, educate them in an art form of their choosing, and celebrate them as the artists they are—and in the process, instill hope and joy.

    President&Creative Director

    Godni Amir Liteman, the founder & creative director of A Free Bird™, is a New York-based singer/songwriter, actor, and makeup artist. Godni was inspired to create A Free Bird, a growing non-profit benefiting artistic children with cancer, after his voice teacher’s husband was diagnosed with cancer. With his strong background in theater, dance, and music, Godni has personally experienced the healing power of the arts.

    Through his music, Godni brought comfort and solace to his voice teacher’s family in their time of greatest need. Today, Godni is committed to bringing that same healing power of the arts to young artists affected by cancer who would otherwise be forced to give up their creative outlets during their medical treatments. Through A Free Bird, Godni hopes to create a healing artistic environment for pediatric cancer patients.

    Godni’s work as a makeup artist has been prominently featured in print, on the runway, and at private events. His national print work includes covers of Women’s Wear Daily; articles in Intimate Magazine and O, The Oprah Magazine; billboards for Curve, the first documentary to feature plus-size models; features for Infatuation, the Mexican fashion periodical; Peggy Jennings catalog; and more.

    Off camera, Godni has worked on looks for U.N. High Commission of Human Rights official Mary Robinson, the Einstein Foundation, and numerous brands during New York Bridal Fashion Week, including Nico & Adrian. He has also served as the artistic director of hair and makeup for Sarantina Bridal and the Tikva Organization by Mark Ecko.

    Godni worked with Bobbi Brown and Chanel, working at various events with her throughout New York City. His celebrity clients through Bobbi Brown include Rihanna, Veanne Cox, Brooklyn Sudano, and photographers Gail Hadani and John Healy.
